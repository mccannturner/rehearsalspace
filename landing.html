How it works

Here's what a typical Rehearsal Space session looks like:

1. Join your band's room

Create a room for your band with a simple link. Set your tempo and time signature. No installs, no accounts required to try it out.

2. Work through sections together

Use the shared metronome to lock in tempo. Record takes of problem sections. Listen back together and decide which version works. Use the best take as backing to rehearse the next part.

3. Show up ready

Walk into your in-person rehearsal or studio session with transitions locked in, tempo agreed upon, and reference recordings ready. Spend your expensive studio time recording, not debating.

Who uses Rehearsal Space

Indie bands recording albums

Lock in arrangements before paying for studio time. A/B test different versions of sections. Save hundreds on rehearsal space fees.

Songwriters demoing ideas

Share early versions of songs with collaborators across the country. Test different tempos and feels before committing to an arrangement.

Worship teams preparing for Sunday

Practice transitions and tempo changes as a team during the week. Show up Sunday morning already aligned and confident.

Probably not for

  • Casual jam sessions with no specific goal
  • Players expecting same-room groove at long distance
  • Large ensembles needing perfect unison hits

Rehearsal Space is built for intentional rehearsal — working toward a session, a show, or a recording. If you just want to hang out and play, that's great! But this probably isn't the right tool.
